Upcoming projects in Fort Bonifacio, Taguig City

Metro Manila Subway Project

The Metro Manila Subway is a 33.1km underground rail project currently being built in Metro Manila, Philippines, linking Valenzuela with Ninoy Aquino International Airport through 17 stations and a depot. As the nation's first underground mass transit system, it is designed to handle up to 1.5 million passengers daily, with an initial capacity of 400,000 to 800,000 passengers. Initiated by the Department of Transportation (DOTr) and approved by the National Economic Development Authority (NEDA), the project is part of the Duterte Administration's Build, Build, Build initiative. The subway's groundbreaking occurred in February 2019, with phase one expected to be finished by 2025 and the entire project slated for completion by July 2027.

Kalayaan Bridge also known as the BGC-Ortigas Link Bridge

The Kalayaan Bridge also known as the BGC-Ortigas Link Bridge, was complete on June 12, 2021. Lawton Avenue and Kapitolyo are connected by this four-lane, two-way bridge that traverses the Pasig River. Its primary objective is to reduce the travel time between Fort Bonifacio and Ortigas to a mere 12 minutes by alleviating traffic congestion on main roadways such as EDSA and C-5. The bridge not only alleviates traffic congestion but also improves connectivity between critical business districts, thereby stimulating economic growth and development in the surrounding region. The enhanced accessibility has also made it more convenient for residents and commuters, contributing to a more efficient transportation network in Metro Manila.

New Senate Of The Philippines

The New Senate of the Philippines was built on a 20,000-sqm (2-hectare) site owned by the Bases Conversion and Development Authority (BCDA), which lies in the Navy Village at Fort Bonifacio. Such a prime location is situated in the rapidly rising Bonifacio Global City (BGC), noted for its modern infrastructure and vibrant business environment. Designed with historical importance but also modernity, it becomes a landmark for good governance. It stands surrounded by high-end housing estates as well as places of culture, signalling our country's aim to be modernizing and progressive. The establishment of this new facility promises to improve the legislative space while at the same time promoting dynamic growth patterns in BGC.

Future developments in Bulacan

New Manila International Airport

The New Manila International Airport will be the biggest and most modern airport outside Metro Manila. This $15-billion initiative of San Miguel Corporation is being built on a 2,565-hectare site in Bulacan, around 35 kilometers from Metro Manila. The airport will feature eight runways, eight taxiways, three terminals, and a capacity to accommodate as many as 100 million passengers annually once fully operational. The airport is conveniently located and will be connected with an 8-kilometer-long toll road and is easily accessible via North Luzon Expressway and the 18-kilometer Skyway Stage 3, which will be connected to the South Luzon Expressway (SLEX). Besides, it is just a 20-minute travel from Megaworld's newest township, Northwin Global City, in Marilao/Bocaue, Bulacan. When it comes to infrastructure, this marks a milestone for the Philippines.

North to South Commuter Railway

NSCR is a large project in the making to improve connectivity and transportation efficiency in Greater Manila Area. The NSCR will build a 38-km circuit connecting Malolos, Bulacan and Tutuban in Manila. The second phase will take the railway 52 kilometers from Malolos to Clark, Pampanga. Moreover, there are plans to open a 56-kilometre rail network in South Luzon, connecting Solis in Manila and Calamba-Laguna. This development will create a sustainable and more complete national railway network, providing reliable, safe transit for the provinces around Metro Manila, benefiting both residents and businesses in the surrounding areas.

Metro Rail Transit Line 7

The Metro Rail Transit Line 7 (MRT-7) is an infrastructure project that will encourage better linkage between Quezon City and San Jose del Monte, Bulacan. This 23-kilometer elevated railway has a total of 14 stations placed at key points to make it the favorite choice for daily commuting, thereby reducing travel time between these two metropolitan centers by approximately half. This growth is expected to benefit not only residents but also businesses, especially in Bulacan, where it would create new employment opportunities and commercial activities. Expected to carry up to 850,000 passengers daily, the MRT 7 project of San Miguel Corporation is an important addition to improving regional public transportation networks. This potentially would boost the residential and commercial property market in the community, thereby adding upside to Megaworld's prospects.
